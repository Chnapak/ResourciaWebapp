About this Resource

This is MIT's 18.06 Linear Algebra course, taught by Professor Gilbert Strang — one of the most acclaimed mathematics educators in the world. The course covers the full spectrum of linear algebra topics essential for engineers, scientists, and mathematicians: vector spaces, linear systems, matrix factorizations (LU, QR, SVD), determinants, eigenvalues and eigenvectors, and applications to differential equations, statistics, and machine learning.

The course materials include 34 lecture videos (each 45–60 min), lecture notes, assignments with solutions, and exams with answer keys. Everything is freely available under MIT's Creative Commons license. The course has been accessed over 10 million times and is considered the gold standard for learning linear algebra online.

Whether you're a CS student preparing for machine learning, an engineer reviewing fundamentals, or a math enthusiast — this course delivers everything you need with exceptional clarity.
